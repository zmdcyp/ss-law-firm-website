Why clients return

Islamabad law firm

Led by Managing Partner Muhammad Junaid Abbasi, S&S Law Firm is a full-service legal & corporate consultancy based in Islamabad, delivering professional legal advice to local businesses, multinationals and private clients across Pakistan.

Our practice covers corporate and commercial, banking and finance, dispute resolution, employment, projects and infrastructure, property and real estate, and international trade. We combine technical depth with a commercial mindset, helping clients navigate complex transactions and high-stakes litigation with clarity and confidence.

When Pakistan's businesses and international clients face complexity, they look for counsel that is responsive, precise and pragmatic—that is what S&S Law Firm is built to deliver.

How we work

Three principles shape every engagement at S&S Law Firm.

01

Principle

We believe ethical process and effective outcomes are not in tension. Our work is grounded in Shariah-compatible structures and transparent conduct — counsel that clients, counterparties and regulators can all stand behind.

02

Precision

Pakistan’s regulatory environment is evolving fast. We bring deep fluency in SECP and NADRA frameworks, NBFC regulation, and commercial practice — documentation that stands up to scrutiny, strategy that anticipates the next turn.

03

Partnership

We act with our clients, not just for them. That means listening before prescribing, pricing with transparency, and measuring ourselves by whether the relationship continues — not whether the matter closes.

Beyond the matter

Engagement & industry leadership

We see our role extending past individual mandates. Our team actively contributes to the institutions, frameworks and conversations that shape Pakistan’s legal and financial future.

Regulatory Engagement

Active working relationships with the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP) and NADRA. We participate in industry consultations and contribute field-tested insight to policy discussions on asset resolution, distressed debt markets, and NBFC regulation.

Shariah-Compliant Finance Infrastructure

Pioneering Islamic-finance-compatible asset resolution frameworks that align recovery with adl, eliminate riba, and offer financial institutions a resolution path that Shariah boards, shareholders and borrowers can all stand behind.

Thought Leadership

Contributing to Pakistan’s inclusive-finance conversation through written analysis, press releases, and sector engagement — with a particular focus on NPL resolution, digital lending regulation, and the legal architecture of financial inclusion.
